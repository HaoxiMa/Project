In terms of per capita income, Connecticut is the richest state in the United States of America. As of a 2010 estimate, Connecticut had a per capita income of $36,775. All data is from the 2010 United States Census and the 2011-2015 American Community Survey 5-Year Estimates.[1][2]

Despite its high per capita income, Connecticut is mainly a middle-class state. Most of Connecticutâ€™s wealth is concentrated in Fairfield County, which contains many affluent suburbs of New York City. Other areas of wealth in the state include suburbs surrounding New Haven and Hartford.
